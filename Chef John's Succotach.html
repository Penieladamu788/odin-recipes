Chef John's Succotash

This may be American's oldest vegetable recipe, coming from a Narraganseft Indian word,
'msickquatash', which, according to the Merrian-Webster Dictionary, means 'boiled corn kernels.' When most of you hear the word
succotash, yam probably think of the catchphrase,'Sufferin' succotash!. But succotash really is a great and very underrated recipe.

Preparation Time

Prep Time:

20 mins

Cook Time:

20 mins

Total Time:

40 mins

Servings

6

Yield

6 servings

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons butter
  • 1/2 yellow onion, dried
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste
  • 1/2 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 jalapeno pepper, sliced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup diced tomatoes
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 pinch cayanne pepper
  • 2 green zucchinis, cut into cubes
  • 4 ounces fresh green beans, cut into 1/2-inch pieces
  • 1/4 cup water
  • 1 1/2 cup corn
  • 1 cup frozen baby lima beans, thawed

Directions

Step 1

Heat olive oil and butter in a skillet over medium. Cook and stir onion with a pinch of salt in hot butter
and oil until onion is softened and slightly golden, 6 to 7 minutes. Add red bell pepper, jalapeno and garic;
cook and stir until peppers are fragant and softened, 3 to 4 minutes.

Step 2

Stir tomatoes, cumin, black peppers, and cayanne pepper into onion mixture; cook and stir until headed through,
1 to 3 mins. Add zucchini, green beans, water, and a pinch of salt; cook until the zucchini are almost tender,
Stir corn and lima beans into zucchini mixture until heated through, about 3 to 5 minutes more. Season with salt.
